NETRA-AI

Netra-AI is a deployable retinal screening platform for automated diabetic retinopathy severity detection using EfficientNet-B5 with Ben-Graham preprocessing, ordinal regression severity prediction, Grad-CAM explainability and test-time augmentation inference. The system supports scalable early screening in primary healthcare environments with limited ophthalmologist availability.

A Crisis of Care

India has over 100 million diabetic patients and faces a severe shortage of ophthalmologists in rural healthcare environments where early retinal screening infrastructure remains limited. Netra-AI enables automated severity grading using portable retinal scans and cloud-based inference pipelines to reduce preventable blindness.

The Trust Gap

Grad-CAM explainability highlights lesion-focused retinal regions enabling transparent doctor-assisted diagnosis and improving clinician confidence in AI-based screening decisions.

Research Contribution

This system integrates Ben-Graham preprocessing, EfficientNet-B5 ordinal regression severity prediction, test-time augmentation stabilization, Grad-CAM explainability and automated retinal image validation filtering into a deployable AI screening workflow.

OUR AI PIPELINE

From Image to Diagnosis

1. Smart Cleaning

Ben-Graham preprocessing removes black borders, normalizes illumination variations, enhances vessel contrast and standardizes retinal images to 456×456 resolution, improving lesion visibility and inference robustness.

2. Deep Analysis

EfficientNet-B5 performs ordinal regression-based severity prediction across five diabetic retinopathy stages using high-resolution retinal inputs with Test-Time Augmentation for stable predictions.

3. Visual Proof

Grad-CAM explainability generates lesion-localization heatmaps highlighting microaneurysms, hemorrhages and vessel abnormalities to support transparent clinical decision-making.

Kappa Score: 0.9155

Near-perfect agreement with ophthalmologist severity grading on the APTOS-2019 dataset. Average inference latency ≈ 2 seconds per retinal scan. Compared to VGG16 and EfficientNet-B3 architectures, EfficientNet-B5 improves lesion sensitivity using higher-resolution feature extraction.
